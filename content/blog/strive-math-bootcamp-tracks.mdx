Not every child who struggles with math is struggling for the same reason. Some are bored. Some have gaps from a difficult year. Some have the ability but have lost confidence after too many experiences of feeling behind. A math bootcamp that tries to serve all of them identically ends up serving none of them well.

Strive's Math Bootcamps are built around two distinct tracks, each designed for a different situation. Understanding which one fits your child is the most important decision before enrolling.

## Math Performance: For Students Who Need Real Challenge

The Performance track is for students who already move through school math comfortably: students who finish assignments quickly, who find the standard curriculum underchallenging, and who need to be stretched rather than supported.

These students often disengage not because they're failing, but because nothing requires them to actually think. They develop habits of rushing, skip showing their working, and then find themselves unstuck when problems genuinely require careful reasoning.

What the Performance track focuses on:

- Advanced problem-solving and competition preparation
- Eliminating careless mistakes through disciplined, methodical working
- Singapore Math methods and global benchmark problem-solving skills

The students who benefit most are in grades 4 to 9, performing above grade level, and looking for problems that actually require them to slow down and think. The goal isn't speed. It's developing the kind of careful reasoning that holds up under exam conditions and beyond.

## Math Confidence: For Students Who've Lost Their Math Spark

The Confidence track starts from a different premise: the student is capable, but something has gone wrong somewhere. Maybe a difficult year with a teacher who moved too fast. Maybe a few foundational concepts that never clicked and have since made everything harder. Maybe simply a loss of belief that they can do it.

Vague report card feedback like "needs to work on fractions" doesn't help. What these students need is a proper diagnosis and a rebuild that makes genuine sense to them.

What the Confidence track delivers:

- A diagnostic assessment to identify actual learning gaps, not just surface symptoms
- Foundation rebuilding that connects to how the student's own school curriculum is structured
- Alignment with what's coming up next, so the progress carries forward into class

The students who benefit most from this track have the ability, and it's visible when you talk through a problem with them, but they've started to believe the story that math just isn't for them. That story is almost always wrong, and the Confidence track is designed to disprove it.

## What Both Tracks Include

Both the Performance and Confidence tracks offer 1:1 personalised online coaching. Each student is matched to an expert instructor based on their specific situation and needs. Sessions are booked flexibly to fit around school and family schedules.

Every programme includes a diagnostic assessment and a 30-minute parent consultation with a Stanford University alumnus, so families understand exactly what the student needs and what to expect.

1:1 instruction matters for a bootcamp. Group classes can't adjust in real time to how an individual student is thinking through a problem. A tutor working one-on-one can meet a student precisely where they are and move at exactly the right pace, without waiting for others to catch up or leaving the student behind.

## Choosing the Right Track

The clearest indicator for the Performance track: your child moves through class work easily and is not being challenged. The clearest indicator for the Confidence track: your child is capable in conversation but shuts down in front of a math problem, or seems to be guessing rather than reasoning.

If you're genuinely unsure, the diagnostic assessment that opens the Confidence track is a useful starting point. It produces a clear picture of where the gaps actually are, which is worth knowing regardless of which direction you go from there.
